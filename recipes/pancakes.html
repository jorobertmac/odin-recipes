Pancakes

Easy, Delicious Pancakes for Your Saturday Morning Tradition

Saturday mornings are meant for slowing down, savoring life, and enjoying the comforting aroma of pancakes sizzling on the griddle. Whether you're' waking up to the laughter of kids or the peaceful quiet of a lazy weekend, pancakes have a way of making the morning feel extra special. Fluffy and golden, these breakfast staples are the perfect canvas for creativity, whether you prefer them drenched in maple syrup, topped with fresh berries, or sprinkled with chocolate chips.

Starting a pancake tradition transforms your Saturday mornings into something truly magical. The process itself—whisking batter, flipping pancakes, and sharing stories over the table—creates memories that last a lifetime. Plus, they're as versatile as they are delicious. You can keep it classic or experiment with fun variations like banana, cinnamon, or even savory pancakes. No matter how you serve them, one thing is certain: pancakes are the ultimate way to bring everyone together to kick off the weekend right.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 1/4 cups milk
  • 1 egg
  • 3 tablespoons melted butter, cooled

Instructions

  1. Pre-heat griddle to medium and lightly oil or spray with cooking spray
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar
  3. Wisk in the milk, egg, and melted butter
  4. Pour the batter on the griddle (about 1/4 cup) and cook for 2-3 minutes until it starts bubbling
  5. Flip the pancake and cook for another 1-2 minutes
  6. Remove and serve immediately or set aside on a wire rack
